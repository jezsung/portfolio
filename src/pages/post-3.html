Splendor, What are Winning Strategies?

What is Splendor?

Splendor is one of the most well-known board games across the world, designed by Marc André and illustrated by Pascal Quidault(Splendor (game), n.d., para. 1). It was published in 2014 by Space Cowboys, Asmodee(Splendor (game), n.d., para. 1). Splendor can be played by 2-4 players and the average playtime is about 30 minutes.

What are the Rules?

Splendor is consist of 3 Elements–Gems, Cards, and Novel Tiles, and players compete with each other to get Cards and Novel Tiles. The players can gain Scores whenever they get Cards or Novel Tiles, and the player who gains the first 15 Scores wins the game.

Each player can perform one possible Action out of three per turn:

  • Take one or more Gems
  • Reserve one Card and take one Gold Gem
  • Purchase Card

Gems are used to purchase Cards. There are 5 different Normal Gems–Emerald, Diamond, Sapphire, Onyx, and Ruby–and 1 Special Gem–Gold. Players can take 3 different Gems or take 2 Gems with the same color–all except for the Gold Gem. Also, players can reserve one Card and take one Gold Gem. Players can treat Gold Gem as any other Gem when purchasing Cards.

Once a player owns the same amount of Gems of the Card that the player wants to purchase, the player can return those Gems and purchase that Card. Players can use their purchased Cards to count the number of Gems they need to return for further purchases. Unlike Gems, purchased Cards will not be returned and can be used infinitely.

Once a player owns the same amount of Cards of the Novel Tiles, that player gets that Novel Tile. Getting Novel Tiles does not consume a player’s turn.

Some Cards and all Novel Tiles have Scores. The players who own those Cards or Novel Tiles get the Scores. If one of the player’s sum of the Scores exceeds 15, that player wins the game.

What are Winning Strategies?

To gain many Scores faster than other players, prioritizing the most valuable Cards is a must. The valuable Cards are the following:

  • Cards that require fewer Gems than others
  • Cards with the Gem that is mostly required by Novel Tiles

Since Novel Tiles will not be refilled once they are taken by other players, the players who own the most Novel Tiles will likely win the game. If you purchase Cards with fewer Gems than others, you will have more Cards than other players which means the more Cards you get, the faster you can get other Cards (Players can purchase Cards with the Gems painted on the Cards they own.) The players who own more Cards are likely to get the Novel Tiles. In conclusion, purchasing Cards with fewer Gems that are required by the Novel Tiles would be the best strategy.
